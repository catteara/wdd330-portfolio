Master Mobile UX Video

Mobile First User Experience

  • Designing for smaller views first and then design for larger screens.
  • This helps to keep thing simple, but still look good.
  • Designs should be responsive, but made on mobile first.
  • This is how I am used to designing, so this won't be an issue.

Improving Ux by Understanding Users

  • Find ways to fill the users needs. Be there, be useful, and be quick.
  • Are the buttons comfortably reachable on the device?
  • Speed Matters. Reduce images, take advantage of SVGs and Webfonts, and optimize, minify, and compress files

Strategic Menu Design

  • Why do apps usually have an easily accessisble navigation, but all websites put their hamburger menus in the top corner, where it is hard to reach?
  • Placement Matters. Reachability, preception of speed, engagement rates, and satisfaction.

Mobile Friendly Forms

  • Display labels within textarea with flost labels.
  • Ask for the least information needed.
  • Taller textareas help with touchability.
  • Enable auto-complete to help out users.
  • Show passwords by default with option to hide.
  • Make the default keyboard what the user needs.
  • Link phone numbers.

Mobile Advertising

  • No pop-up ads!
